Identifying the Signs of a Raccoon Infestation in Delano

Raccoons are nocturnal creatures, meaning they are most active at night. This can make it difficult to spot them directly, but they often leave behind clear signs of their presence. Recognizing these signs early can help you address a potential problem before it escalates. If you notice any of the following indicators on your Delano property, it might be time to consider calling in the professionals for raccoon removal.

Strange Noises at Night

Scratching and Thumping Sounds

One of the most common signs of a raccoon infestation, especially in attics or walls, is hearing unusual noises during the night. Raccoons are relatively large animals and can be quite active once the sun goes down. You might hear scratching, thumping, or scurrying sounds as they move around in these enclosed spaces. These sounds can be particularly noticeable in quiet hours and might sound like something is walking or dragging itself across surfaces. The location and intensity of the sounds can often give clues to where the raccoons are nesting or entering your home. While squirrels can also make noise in attics, raccoon noises are often deeper and heavier due to their size. Don't ignore these sounds; they are a strong indication that an unwanted guest has taken up residence on your property.

Growling or Hissing

In addition to movement sounds, you might also hear vocalizations from raccoons, particularly if they are disturbed or if there are young present. Adult raccoons can make a variety of sounds, including growling, hissing, chattering, and even screaming. If you hear these kinds of noises coming from your attic, chimney, or other enclosed areas, it's highly probable that you have raccoons. Female raccoons with kits are especially protective and might vocalize if they feel threatened. Hearing these sounds from within your home can be unsettling and confirms the need for wildlife control experts to safely remove the animals. It's important to avoid confronting raccoons directly, especially when they are vocalizing, as they can become aggressive.

Damage to Your Property

Overturned Garbage Cans

Raccoons are notorious for their opportunistic feeding habits, and easily accessible trash cans are a prime target. If you consistently find your garbage cans overturned, lids removed, and trash scattered around your property, raccoons are likely the culprits. They are strong enough to tip over even heavy cans and will leave a trail of debris as they rummage for food scraps. This is not only unsightly but can also attract other pests like ants and cockroaches. Securing your garbage cans with heavy-duty lids or straps can help deter raccoons, but persistent issues indicate a stronger need for professional intervention.

Damage to Roof, Siding, or Vents

Raccoons are surprisingly strong and determined, and they can cause significant damage to your home's exterior in their efforts to gain entry. They may tear at roofing materials like shingles, fascia boards, or ventilation covers. They might also chew or claw at wood or siding to create or enlarge openings. Chimneys without proper caps are also a common entry point. Inspecting your roof and the exterior of your home for signs of damage like torn materials, enlarged gaps, or claw marks is important in identifying potential entry points. These damaged areas can also lead to water damage and other structural issues if not addressed promptly. Professional inspection by a pest control professional in Delano is highly recommended to assess the extent of the damage and develop a plan for repair and exclusion.

Ransacked Gardens or Bird Feeders

If you have a garden, particularly one with fruits or vegetables, you might find signs of raccoons helping themselves to your harvest. They will eat ripe produce and can cause considerable damage to plants as they forage. Similarly, bird feeders are a favorite target due to the readily available food source. Raccoons are adept at climbing and can access even hanging feeders, often knocking them down or tearing them open. Finding empty or damaged feeders and scattered birdseed can be a strong indicator of raccoon activity on your property. While frustrating, this type of damage is a clear sign that raccoons have identified your yard as a food source and may be looking for shelter nearby.

Other Indicative Signs

Distinctive Animal Droppings

Raccoon droppings have a distinctive appearance and are often found in concentrated areas known as latrines. These latrines are typically located in elevated areas like rooftops, decks, or in attics. Raccoon scat is usually tubular, blunt-ended, and may contain visible remnants of what they've been eating, such as seeds or berries. It's crucial to exercise caution when cleaning up raccoon droppings, as they can contain harmful parasites and bacteria, including raccoon roundworm, which can be dangerous to humans and pets. Wearing gloves and a mask is recommended, and professional cleanup is often the safest option due to the health risks involved. Identifying these latrines is a key factor in confirming a raccoon presence and understanding their movement patterns on your property.

Unpleasant Odors

The presence of raccoons can also be detected by offensive odors. Raccoon urine has a strong, musky smell that can permeate affected areas, especially in enclosed spaces like attics or crawl spaces. Over time, the accumulation of droppings and urine can create a very unpleasant and persistent odor. You might also notice the smell of decay if a raccoon has died within a wall or other inaccessible area. These odors are not only unpleasant but can also attract other pests. Addressing the source of the odor through professional cleanup and removal is essential for restoring the air quality and comfort of your home. A lingering foul smell that you can't identify the source of should prompt investigation for potential wildlife activity.

Tracks or Paw Prints

Raccoons have five toes on each foot and their paw prints are somewhat similar to small human hands, particularly the front paws. You might find their tracks in soft soil, mud, or dust around your property, especially near entry points or food sources. The tracks often show distinct finger-like toes with noticeable claw marks. Looking for these tracks can help you confirm the presence of raccoons and also indicate their travel paths around your home. Seeing these distinctive prints, especially near damaged areas or garbage cans, provides strong evidence of raccoon activity and the need for professional intervention to prevent further issues.

Ignoring these signs can lead to more significant damage and health risks over time. Prompt action is key to a successful resolution.

Addressing Common Raccoon Issues in Delano Properties

Raccoons are highly adaptable animals, and they can cause a variety of problems for homeowners in Delano. Their behavior is often driven by their need for food, water, and shelter. Understanding the specific ways raccoons can impact your property can help you recognize the problem and seek appropriate assistance. Here are some of the most common issues we address when dealing with raccoon removal in Delano, CA.

Raccoons in the Attic

Finding raccoons in your attic is a serious concern. Attics provide a warm, dry, and safe haven for raccoons, especially female raccoons looking for a place to raise their young. Once in the attic, they can cause extensive damage. They will tear up insulation for nesting material, chew on electrical wiring (creating a fire hazard), and contaminate the area with urine and droppings. The noise they make can also be very disruptive to your sleep and peace of mind. Addressing raccoons in the attic requires careful and humane removal, followed by thorough cleanup, sanitization, and importantly, sealing off all entry points to prevent future infestations. Our professional team is experienced in safely removing raccoons and their young from attics without causing further damage to your home.

Raccoons in Chimneys

Chimneys are another common spot where raccoons might decide to set up a home. An uncapped chimney provides an easy entry point, and the vertical structure offers a sense of security. Raccoons, particularly females with kits, will climb down chimneys to nest. This can lead to blockages, fire hazards, and the potential for raccoons to enter your living space if they fall or climb down into your fireplace. Removing raccoons from a chimney requires specialized tools and techniques to ensure both the safety of the animal and your home. Once removed, installing a sturdy chimney cap is essential to prevent future instances. Our technicians are equipped to handle chimney removals safely and effectively.

Raccoons Under Decks or Sheds

Outdoor structures like decks and sheds can also offer attractive shelter for raccoons, providing protection from the elements and predators. Raccoons nesting under these structures can cause damage by digging and burrowing. Their presence can also create unpleasant odors and attract other pests. Removing raccoons from these locations often involves trapping and then securing the perimeter of the structure to prevent re-entry. This might involve installing wire mesh or other barriers to block off access points. Our team can assess the situation and recommend the most effective exclusion methods for your specific deck or shed.

Raccoons Raiding Garbage and Outdoor Food Sources

While raccoons entering your home is a major concern, their activity in your yard can also be a significant nuisance. As mentioned earlier, overturned garbage cans are a classic sign of raccoons. They are also attracted to pet food left outdoors, bird feeders, gardens, and even compost piles. This foraging behavior can create a mess and attract other wildlife. Managing outdoor food sources is a critical part of preventing raccoon problems. This includes securing garbage cans, bringing pet food indoors at night, and potentially using critter-proof bird feeders. Our team can provide advice on best practices for minimizing attractants on your property in Delano.

Concerns About Diseases and Aggression

Beyond the physical damage they can cause, raccoons can also pose health risks. They can carry diseases like rabies, distemper, and the raccoon roundworm, which can be transmitted to humans and pets. While raccoons typically avoid confrontation, they can become aggressive if cornered or if they feel their young are threatened. It's important to maintain a safe distance from raccoons and never attempt to handle them yourself. If you suspect a raccoon on your property is sick or behaving strangely (e.g., active during the day, appearing disoriented, or exhibiting aggressive behavior), it's crucial to contact (661) 462-2431 immediately for professional assistance. Our team prioritizes safety and will handle the removal of potentially sick or aggressive animals with caution and expertise.

Addressing any sign of raccoon activity early is crucial to prevent the problem from escalating. Waiting too long can lead to more extensive damage, higher costs for removal and repair, and increased health risks.
